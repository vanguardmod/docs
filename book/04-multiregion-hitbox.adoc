[[multiregion-hitbox]]
= Multi-Region Hitbox

The {vg-name} hit-detection model is built on per-region capsules attached to
the player's animation skeleton, replacing the single axis-aligned bounding
box (AABB) used by stock {et-name}. This is the mod's flagship technical
feature and the foundation of every weapon-balance decision.

The capsule set and impactpoint indices documented below are stable as of
v0.5.2.3. Diagrams and per-region damage scaling tables are deferred to a
later docs revision.

== Overview

Stock ET resolves hits against a 36×72-unit AABB that follows the player
origin. The AABB is cheap, but it has well-known problems: hits register
visibly off-model, and hit-region differentiation (head/body/legs) is
approximated rather than measured.

{vg-name} replaces this with a stack of bone-anchored capsules — one per
hit region — populated each server frame from the same skeletal data the
renderer uses to draw the player. A bullet trace is tested against each
capsule in priority order, and the first intersection determines both
the damage scaling and the impactpoint reported to the client.

== Hit regions

The current capsule set covers nine logical regions:

[cols="1,1,3",options="header",frame=all]
|===
| Region   | Impactpoint | Notes

| HEAD     | 1
| Sphere on the skull (Bip01 Head bone), `+6.5` Z-offset to skull centre,
  radius `7` (was `6` prior to v0.5.2.3).

| NECK     | 2
| Sphere with radius `4`, bridging head and chest (gap-fill added in
  v0.5.2.3 to close the "neck-shot pass-through" gap).

| CHEST    | 3
| Capsule from sternum to upper-abdomen.

| GUT      | 4
| Capsule from upper-abdomen to pelvis.

| GROIN    | 5
| Sphere at the pelvis (groin-hit capsule).

| SHOULDER | 6
| Two capsules, one per shoulder, bridging neck and upper-arm bones.

| ARMS     | 7
| Four capsules — UpperArm and Forearm, left and right (added in v0.5.2.3).

| KNEE     | 8
| Two capsules, one per leg, around the knee bone.

| LEGS     | 9
| Two capsules, one per leg, from hip to ankle.
|===

Impactpoint `0` (`IMPACTPOINT_UNUSED`) is reserved for "no region matched".
With <<cvar.vanguard_hitbox_strict>> at its default `1`, a shot that
resolves to impactpoint `0` deals no damage; with strict disabled, a shot
that resolves to `0` falls back to the legacy AABB.

== Strict vs legacy mode

The cvar <<cvar.vanguard_hitbox_strict>> controls whether hits that miss every
capsule fall back to the legacy AABB:

* `vanguard_hitbox_strict 1` *(default, cup-orthodox)* — capsule miss = no
  damage. There is no fallback. This is the only correct setting for
  competitive play.
* `vanguard_hitbox_strict 0` — capsule miss falls back to the legacy AABB.
  This is provided for legacy-compatibility testing only; cup matches **must
  not** run with this setting.

== Lag compensation and multi-region

Lag compensation is the server-side technique that rewinds the world to
the moment the shooter actually pulled the trigger, so a shot fired at a
target the shooter saw on their screen also hits that target on the
server. In stock {et-name} the rewind only needs to restore the position
of each player's AABB — a single origin and a fixed-shape box.

Multi-region capsules raise the cost of a correct rewind: a faithful
replay needs to restore not just the target's origin but also the
animation state that drove each capsule's bone, because every region
moves independently when the player's skeleton animates (running, prone,
crouch transition, weapon swap). A naive origin-only rewind correctly
predicts torso hits but mispredicts limb hits whenever the limb has
moved relative to the torso during the lag-comp window.

== Diagnostics

When debugging hit-detection in production, the cvar
<<cvar.vanguard_hitbox_debug>> emits `VG_DIAG:` log lines for every shot,
including bone resolution and capsule-test results. A separate one-shot
diagnostic dump (<<cvar.vanguard_diag_dump>>) gives a full bone-axis
world-projection at the next eligible shot.

The two log prefixes are:

`VG_DIAG:`::
  One per shot, written for every bullet trace while
  <<cvar.vanguard_hitbox_debug>> is `1`. Includes the shooter slot, the
  victim slot, the resolved capsule (or "none" for a strict-mode reject),
  and the impactpoint number that will be reported back to the client.

`VG_DIAG_DUMP:`::
  Multi-line burst, written once when <<cvar.vanguard_diag_dump>> is
  armed and a subsequent eligible shot fires. Contains the full
  bone-axis world-projection at the moment of the shot — useful for
  reconstructing the capsule geometry against a demo timeline.

To extract just the diagnostic stream from a busy server log:

[source,console]
----
$ grep -E '^VG_DIAG(_DUMP)?:' server.log
----

[CAUTION]
====
The diagnostic output is verbose and is **not appropriate for long-running
production servers** — the per-shot rate scales with player count and
weapon fire rate. Enable it for the duration of a test, then disable it.
The `VG_DIAG_DUMP:` burst is a one-shot and self-disables; the per-shot
`VG_DIAG:` stream stays on until <<cvar.vanguard_hitbox_debug>> is
explicitly set back to `0`.
====

See also: <<cvar.vanguard_hitbox_strict>>, <<cvar.vanguard_hitbox_debug>>,
<<cvar.vanguard_diag_dump>>.
